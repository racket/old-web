  Merely *specifying* a Full extended from Advanced is a huge pain. And
  then there's the implementation task. Here's an extract of an earlier
  message on this topic:

  At Mon, 10 Dec 2001, Matthew Flatt wrote:
  > return to my old definition: it's MrEd + selected MzLib libraries, such
  > that all the syntactic forms and procedures of Advanced are available
  > (plus a lot more). But the forms act like they do in MrEd + MzLib when
  > they might produce an error (syntactic for run-time) in Advanced.
  >
  > In particular,
  >  (cond)
  > isn't an error, and neither is
  >  (cond [#f 5])
  > or
  >  (cond [5 5])
  > --- not because # is the best behavior in the first two cases,
  > but because that's how MrEd works.
  >
  > The alternative --- to make Full's syntactic forms behave more like
  > Advanced --- is a significant specification and implementation task.
  > It's not just `cond' and `case'; `local' might signal undefined
  > variable uses as an error, which suggests that `class' should do the
  > same, and so on.
