Floating-point and Rational Numbers in DrScheme

To avoid explaining floating-point numbers to students right away, we will disallow decimal numbers as input in DrScheme's Beginner language. (The interim solution of converting decimal inputs to exact numbers was rejected on the grounds that if two different language levels both accept an expression as legal, they should assign it the same semantics.)

In case the student uses a transcendental function, such as sqrt the decimal result will be printed with a leading #i, and decimals prefixed with #i will be accepted as input. This will enable a student to step through computations.

A related question is how to print non-integer rational numbers. Humans are likely to find 21/2 more readable than 5/2. DrScheme could print 5/2 as (+ 2 1/2), but this may induce as much confusion as clarification. (Do teachers understand that 21/2 means 2+1/2? Will the rewriting rules be confusing?) The solution, for a future release, is that DrScheme will print 21/2 using the editor's graphics capabilities. This printed output can be cut-and-pasted to embed it in a program.
